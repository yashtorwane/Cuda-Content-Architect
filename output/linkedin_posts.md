# LinkedIn Posts based on Rick Astley's "Never Gonna Give You Up"

## Post 1
"A full commitment's what I'm thinking of."

We treat customer retention like an afterthought, but it should be the foundation of your business.

True loyalty isn't just about offering a good product. It’s about promising unwavering devotion and actually following through. When you vow to never give up on your clients, you build a relationship that lasts. 

Don't just sell. Commit. How are you showing up for your long-term clients today?

## Post 2
"You know the rules and so do I."

Clear expectations are the bedrock of any successful partnership. 

Too many teams operate in the dark, hoping the other side gets it. But when there is a deep, mutual understanding between two individuals—whether it's a vendor and a client, or a manager and a direct report—friction disappears.

Stop guessing. State the rules early. Play the game together.

## Post 3
"We're no strangers to love."

Relationships take time to build, but they are the fastest way to grow your business.

The best partnerships are built on a long-standing connection. When you invest time to truly understand your client's aches and pains, you stop being just another vendor. 

You become irreplaceable. Take the time to build real connections this week.

## Post 4
Integrity is your best marketing strategy.

A firm promise to never cause pain, tell a lie, or abandon your partner isn't just a good lyric—it's a business model. 

Clients remember how you handle crises more than how you handle successes. Be the person who never lets them down, especially when things get tough. 

Trust is hard to earn and easy to lose. Guard it.

## Post 5
How do you stand out in a crowded market?

Simple: Distinguish yourself from "any other guy" through sheer reliability.

A direct, earnest plea for your partner to recognize your genuine commitment works wonders. But you have to back it up. If you promise to never run around and desert them, mean it.

Consistency is the ultimate differentiator. What's your core promise?
